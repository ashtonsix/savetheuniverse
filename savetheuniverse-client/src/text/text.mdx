<h2 id="pseudo-momentum">Defining pseudo-momentum</h2>

We employ a toy simulation to introduce pseudo-momentum, a novel quantity derived from the introduction of pseudo-elastic collisions. While the simulation does not model any known physical laws, it provides a clear demonstration of emergent behaviour related to pseudo-momentum.

In our simulation particles follow straight-line paths at constant velocity. Collisions with the boundary are perfectly elastic, while particle-particle collisions are pseudo-elastic. All particles share equal mass.

The equation governing particle-particle collisions is given by:

$$
\displaylines{
	\mathbf{J} = \frac{(1 + E)(\mathbf{x}_1 - \mathbf{x}_2)}{2} \frac{\langle \mathbf{v}_1 - \mathbf{v}_2, \mathbf{x}_1 - \mathbf{x}_2\rangle}{\langle \mathbf{x}_1 - \mathbf{x}_2, \mathbf{x}_1 - \mathbf{x}_2\rangle}, \\
	{\color{blue}S = \frac{\|\mathbf{v}_1\| + \|\mathbf{v}_2\|}{\|\mathbf{v}_1 - \mathbf{J}\| + \|\mathbf{v}_2 + \mathbf{J}\|}}, \\
	\mathbf{v}'_1 = {\color{blue}S} (\mathbf{v}_1 - \mathbf{J}), \\
	\mathbf{v}'_2 = {\color{blue}S} (\mathbf{v}_2 + \mathbf{J}),
}
$$

where $\mathbf{x}$ and $\mathbf{v}$ denote position and velocity respectively, $\mathbf{J}$ is the impulse, $E$ is the coefficient of pseudo-elasticity, and ${\color{blue}S}$ is a scaling factor introduced to preserve pseudo-momentum. In the absence of ${\color{blue}S}$, the equation reduces to the classical particle-particle collision equation.

For $E \lt 1$, particles' velocities align, exhibiting emergent order. This simulation permits a reduction of disorder within a closed system, unlike physical processes that only produce a superficial reduction of disorder (such as the formation of our solar system). This is because kinetic energy is not converted into internal energy.

With or without ${\color{blue}S}$, the direction of total momentum remains invariant. For $E = 1$, the following conservation symmetries are observed:

$$
\displaylines {
  \|\mathbf{p}_1\| + \|\mathbf{p}_2\| = \|\mathbf{p}'_1\| + \|\mathbf{p}'_2\|, \\
  \mathbf{p}_1 + \mathbf{p}_2 = \mathbf{p}'_1 + \mathbf{p}'_2,
}
$$

where $\mathbf{p}$ is (pseudo-)momentum. Classical physics loses symmetry $\|\mathbf{p}_1\| + \|\mathbf{p}_2\| = \|\mathbf{p}'_1\| + \|\mathbf{p}'_2\|$ for $E \neq 1$. However, the inclusion of ${\color{blue}S}$ retains it, leading us to define pseudo-momentum as this conserved quantity. In adopting pseudo-momentum, we forfeit the conservation of momentum $\mathbf{p}_1 + \mathbf{p}_2 = \mathbf{p}'_1 + \mathbf{p}'_2$ and time-reversibility for $E \neq 1$. Nevertheless, this sacrifice is necessary in our quest to challenge the second law of thermodynamics.
